📊 How Scores Are Calculated
Important: The score measures corporate PAC funding — not corruption. A high score means a member receives significant money from corporate PACs and industry groups. A low score means they are primarily grassroots-funded. It is not a legal or moral judgment.
What the score actually calculates

For members who take corporate PAC money, the score starts at 50 and adds equal points per sector based on verified amounts:

Any amount
+3 pts
Over $500K
+6 pts
Over $2M
+9 pts
Applied equally to: AIPAC · Oil & Gas · Pharma · Finance · Defense · Tech and overall career corporate total. Max score: 94.
Members with $0 corporate PAC

Members with verified $0 corporate PAC pledges use a manually-reviewed baseline score based on ethics history and public record — typically 8–35. FEC industry employment data is never used to inflate these scores.

SCOTUS & Cabinet

Scores are set manually based on documented ethics disclosures, ProPublica reporting, confirmed gifts, and conflicts of interest. The PAC formula does not apply.

Score ranges
0 – 35 🟢No corporate PAC money or minimal verified amounts 36 – 65 🟡Some corporate PAC presence 66 – 100 🔴Significant corporate PAC, AIPAC, or industry money
Data sources
  • FEC — Federal Election Commission campaign finance records
  • OpenSecrets — Career sector donation totals
  • TrackAIPAC.com — Verified AIPAC PAC contributions
  • ProPublica — SCOTUS financial disclosures
  • Congress.gov / GovTrack / Bioguide — Voting records, biographical data
Limitations
  • Career totals may not reflect recent changes in fundraising behavior
  • Some newer members have limited FEC records
  • Scores for members without donation data include editorial judgment
  • This formula is our own composite — not an official government rating
The Corporate Money Score measures PAC and industry donation amounts only — not ethics, character, or ideology. Ethics concerns, legal issues, and conflicts of interest are documented separately in each member's Controversies tab.
