About

Donald J. Trump is the 45th and 47th President of the United States, the first person in American history to serve two non-consecutive terms. A real estate developer and television personality, he hosted The Apprentice from 2004 to 2015 before launching his political career. He won the 2016 presidential election over Hillary Clinton despite losing the popular vote, then lost the 2020 election to Joe Biden, which he refused to concede, claiming without evidence that the election was stolen. His first term was marked by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017, withdrawal from the Paris Climate Agreement, and two impeachments, neither resulting in removal. After the January 6, 2021 Capitol attack, he was impeached a second time for incitement of insurrection. He was indicted on 91 criminal counts across four cases in 2023-2024; all federal charges were dropped after his 2024 re-election. He won the 2024 election with 312 electoral votes and was inaugurated as the 47th President on January 20, 2025.
